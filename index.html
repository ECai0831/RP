Effects of low temperatures on soluble sugar concentration & protein synthesis in Opuntia humifusa

Background

Opuntia humifusa is a cold-hardy cactus that survives in temperatures of 0°C and below. This study aimed to explore the molecular responses that allow for such cold resistance. Under low temperatures, cold-induced proteins and soluble sugars assist cell function and prevent ice formation.

Rationale

This study was conducted to investigate the influence of different low temperatures on the protein synthesis and soluble sugar contents of a cold-hardy plant. The molecular responses toward cold stress demonstrate biochemical adaptations to low temperatures and provide a better understanding of plant stress response and freeze tolerance.
